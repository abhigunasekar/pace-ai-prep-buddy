Subject: Request for feedback on Butler Lumber PACE prep prototype

Professor Baker,

I built a local MVP of PACE, an AI-enabled FIN1 preparation tool, using Butler Lumber Day 1 as the pilot case.

The prototype is designed around one constraint: it should improve preparation without replacing student thinking. Students move through a gated flow: review the relevant tools, complete tutorial checks, answer the assignment questions, confirm when Excel work is required, explain their reasoning, and submit a pre-class reflection. The instructor view shows submission timing, average completion time, and the questions where students report getting stuck.

I also built a first-pass Excel taskpane helper. It gives process guidance on selected cells, sources and uses logic, cash conversion cycle setup, and modeling workflow. It intentionally does not complete the model for the student.

I would value your feedback on three questions:

1. Is this the right student flow for improving class preparation?
2. Which Butler Lumber questions or concepts should the tutor handle differently?
3. What evidence would you want from a pilot before considering this for a broader FIN1 rollout?

If useful, I can walk you through the local prototype and then convert it into a Canvas-ready pilot for one upcoming case.
